10-20 minutes daily. Take it slow.

90:90 Transitions 10 reps
Cue Slow and pain-free. Back knee leads, pelvis follows.
Setup Seated on floor. 90° angle at each knee.
Do Use your hands for support. Back knee opens out to the side, pelvis turns with it, front knee follows. Land in 90:90 on the other side. Repeat back and forth.
Why Hip rotation mobility.
Bridge March 2 x 20s
Cue Keep the walnut clenched. March the feet. Hips level — no rocking side to side.
Do Hold bridge position, then alternate lifting each foot slightly off the ground. Keep hips level throughout.
Why Glute endurance and pelvic stability.
Side-Lying Hip Abduction 15 ea
Cue Straight leg — don't let your pelvis roll back. Feel the side of your hip.
Do Side-lying, bottom knee bent for stability. Lift the top leg straight up toward the ceiling. Lower slowly.
Why Strengthens the outside of the hip — your weaker side is the right.
Single Leg Balance 2-3 ea
Cue Stand tall. No hands — you've got this.
Do Stand on one leg. Hold 30 seconds each side.
Why Balance and hip stability.
Hip Hikes + Reach 10 ea
Cue Press down on stance leg. Floating leg lifts and locks. Opposite arm reaches up.
Do Stand on one leg (stance leg). Press down through it, let the other leg hang, then lift and lock the hip higher. As the hip lifts, reach the opposite arm overhead.
Why Hip stability and glute med strengthening.
Calf Raises 10-15
Cue Toes about 3 feet from the wall. Slow up, slow down.
Do Stand facing a wall, toes about 3 feet away. Rise up onto your toes slowly, then lower back down slowly.
Why Ankle strength and balance support.
Stork Squat 8 ea
Cue Only go down to where you can keep your pelvis level. Knee over little toe, hips level — imagine a tray of drinks. Stay tall. Slow on the way down — that's the gold. Use a mirror for feedback.
Do Stand on one leg. Slowly lower into a single-leg squat. Drive through your heel to stand back up.
Why Single-leg strength and pelvic stability under load.
Note: In the video, the model goes very deep. For you, only go as deep as you can keep your pelvis level — straight down, no lateral movement.
Bed Stretch 30-60s ea
Cue Relax the hanging leg. Find a position that gives you a stretch in the front of the hip.
Do Lie on back at edge of bed. Pull one knee to chest, let the other leg hang off the side. Hold 30-60 seconds each side. Can also do slow reps — let it hang, bring it up, repeat.
Why Lengthens the hip flexor.
Marches any time
Cue Lift knee, controlled lower. Light and easy.
Do Standing tall, alternate lifting each knee up toward your chest. Controlled and deliberate — one leg at a time.
Why Hip flexor activation, balance, movement snack.
Chicken Arm Circles 10 ea dir
Cue Work within a comfortable range. Don't push through.
Do Stand or sit. Make small arm circles in each direction. Keep the movement easy and within a range that feels comfortable.
Why Shoulder mobility, upper body movement snack.
Wrist Circles 30s ea
Cue Easy and gentle. Keep them moving.
Do Make gentle circles with each wrist. Both directions. Great while watching TV.
Why Wrist mobility, easy movement snack.
Ape Feet 10 pulses
Cue Spread the floor with your feet. Great while watching TV.
Do Standing or sitting, spread your toes and press them into the floor. Tripod contact — big toe, little toe, heel. Pulse and release.
Why Foot activation and balance support.
Walking 5x/wk
Cue Rest before it hurts. You're doing great — keep building.
Do Use the interval format (2-3 min walk / 1 min rest) as your baseline. Real-world walking is encouraged too — errands, shopping, whatever gets you moving. Stop before aching starts, even if you feel fine. 5x/week minimum — listen to music, keep it fun!
Why Building walking tolerance without triggering the pain cycle.
